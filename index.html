01 — ML-driven architecture

ECHOANA

A wellness-recovery residence where room assignment is usually arbitrary. ECHOANA replaces that guesswork with a two-stage pipeline — k-means discovers four therapeutic room profiles directly from patient data, a classifier generalizes them to new admissions, and a recovery regressor drives an adaptive facade that eases open as each patient heals.

Four therapeutic signatures

Group A
4 single rooms, south — max all-day sun, nearest to care. Best for low vitamin D + high physical need.
Group B
3 double rooms, west — strong afternoon sun, for sun-seeking patients with a caretaker.
Group C
1 double room, north — soft diffuse light, for light-sensitive patients with a caretaker.
Group D
2 single rooms, north/courtyard — garden view, where a biophilic view outperforms direct sun.

02 — Individual project

Circadian-Responsive Hospital Room Design

76% of inpatient studies report poor sleep quality and insufficient duration. This project asks a performative-design question: can room geometry and window size be generated from a circadian light target instead of a floor-plan template — and does that regulate melatonin well enough to matter clinically?

Melanopic lux calculator

Melanopic lux — the metric that actually drives the circadian clock — isn't raw brightness. It's photopic lux scaled by a melanopic daylight efficacy ratio (mDER) that depends on the light's spectrum.

440melanopic lux
Melanopic lux = photopic lux × mDER. Daytime targets sit well above 250 melanopic lux to entrain the clock; evening exposure is kept low by switching to warm-white sources with a lower mDER — exactly the logic the room geometry is optimized against.

Orientation logic

North
Diffuse daylight, no direct sun → highest facade perforation.
East
Soft morning sun → medium–high perforation.
South
Strong, near-constant sun exposure → lowest perforation.
West
Harsh afternoon sun → controlled, tightened perforation.

A Galapagos genetic solver evolves the perforation domain per orientation, with fitness set to maximize spatial Daylight Autonomy (sDA) from Honeybee — so identical orientations end up with different facades once real site context and shading are accounted for.

03 — Team project · New Giza University

Park Tech NGU

NGU's campus parking was informal, unshaded and unsafe — no navigation, no space discipline, no relief from Cairo's heat. Park Tech NGU redesigns the lot as a sensing structure: ultrasonic bays, an RFID gate, and a canopy of kinetic louvers that track the sun and fold open and closed across the day.

Site & problem statement

NGU's parking suffers from poor organization, inefficient space usage and limited safety — causing congestion, unauthorized parking and daily inconvenience. The brief called for a smart intervention: better layout, real-time monitoring, and intelligent shading for cars and people.

75 students were surveyed. 84% see cars parked outside their lane every day or multiple times a week; 69% wanted a digital map of available spots before anything else.

What the survey actually said

"Please put lines on the ground — people leave so much space they take two spots with one car." · "Add convex mirrors at intersections and speed bumps." · "People should park correctly!!"

Five subsystems, one board

Smart shading

LDR + servo motors auto-adjust the louvers to sun intensity.

Car detection

Ultrasonic sensor + red/green LED per bay shows availability at a glance.

RFID access gate

RFID reader + motorized arm automates and secures entry/exit.

Climate-controlled waiting area

Temperature sensor + fan keep the waiting zone livable in summer.

Data logging

SD-card module records light, temperature and occupancy for monitoring.

Louver response, live

The Grasshopper definition reads an LDR-equivalent light value and rotates each louver toward the sun vector. Drag to see the canopy respond.

At low intensity the louvers lie flat to admit light and warmth; as intensity climbs they rotate shut, tracking the sun vector from date and location — the same logic driving the real servo in the physical prototype.

How the script works

An offset surface is gridded into louver strips, each rotated by a distance-to-attractor value remapped against a live sun path (Ladybug ImportEPW + SunPath). The center point acts like the physical LDR: maximum alignment with the sun vector means maximum exposure — exactly the angle that drives the servo on the built prototype.
